General Administration and Scoring Direction
  1. Administer the items in the order in which they appear on the Record Form.
  2. Abbrevieated administration and scoring directions appear on the Record Form. For complete, item-specific administration and scoring directions, refer to the Examiner's Manual
  3. Repetition of some items is allowed. Those items are noted on the Record Form and in the Examiner's Manual.
  4. Do not penalize the child for a delay in response (approximately 5 seconds) to any of the items.
  5. If necessary, on items where the child is supposed to imitate your model, say "My turn" while pointing to yourself and "Your turn" while pointing to the child so he or she will understand the turn-taking task.

General Motor Control Score Descriptions

The following score descriptions are general. Refer to the Examiner's Manual. for item-specific score descriptions

  • 2 = Movement is precise in all parameters.
  • 1 = Movement is partially imprecise in one or more parameters.
  • 0 = Movement is severely imprecise in one or more parameters.

If the child achieves a score of 2 in the Auditory or Visual columns, enter a score of 2 in the remaining column(s). If the child does not respond, write NR next to the column and score as 0.
